The Context

India has a large number of local social entrepreneurs and grassroots initiatives working on various social issues in rural and urban areas. They play a significant role in shaping the social fabric of the nation, tackling grassroots challenges and bringing government schemes and programmes to the underserved communities. This role was duly acknowledged and appreciated by Governments and Development Actors across the globe during the Covid-19 pandemic, when the grassroots initiatives and social enterprises effectively provided last mile delivery of services.

However, these social entrepreneurs and enterprises face several challenges that hinder their ability to bring sustainable change on the ground including lack of structured approach in their work and in presenting their work, dearth of resources and inability to access and tap locally available and in-country resources. In addition, with India emerging as a growing economic power, international donors too have started investing in other countries.

On the other hand, there are many rising opportunities. The Government of India is proactively leading the transformation of the social landscape by reaching marginalized communities through social development schemes, programmes and enabling legal mechanisms. India’s middle-class segment is also growing, along with rise in charitable giving. Additionally, being the first nation to legalize corporate social responsibility, India is providing a structured boost to development endeavours at the grassroots.

Smile Foundation, in alignment with ‘Atmanirbhar Bharat’, has been working to empower social grassroots initiatives as well as social entrepreneurs towards self-reliance and sustainability. Empowering Grassroots is rooted in the belief that it is important to develop capacities and harness the capabilities of social change makers and grassroots initiatives, in turn enabling them to address the needs of the civil society by supplementing and complementing the efforts of the government and in line with the global development strategies, for gaining resilience, future readiness, credibility and local support.

About Us

Built on Smile Foundation’s 20 years of extensive experience working with social entrepreneurs and local initiatives in India, Empowering Grassroots brings an India focused program to strengthen the capabilities of social change makers and grassroots organizations towards better governance, strengthened leadership, resilience and sustainability. The program enables them to gain credibility and support. It also helps them become self-sufficient by honing their skills in identifying, accessing and mobilizing locally available resources.

Empowering Grassroots Initiative launched for capacity building of NGOs/CBOs.

2008-2013

Partnered with Wilde Ganzen Foundation to scale up the SVP projects. Joined Together for Change Alliance.

Completed training for 500 Grassroots level Organizations from India.

Launch of Change the Game Academy global program. Co-created the classroom course of Local Fundraising and Mobilising Support. Chosen as Nodal partner for Asia region for CtGA

Adapted classroom courses into virtual courses. A versatile pool of Trainers and Dedicated Team in place

2021

Training Initiatives Consolidated as Change the Game India. Completed training for 710 grassroot level organizations globally. A versatile pool of trainers and a dedicated team in place.

OUR MENTORS

Mr. Santanu Mishra

Mr Santanu Mishra, a law graduate, a Company Secretary, and an alumnus of the Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad (IIM-A), specializes in strategic management, enterprise transformation and Institution building. After an illustrious career in the corporate sector spanning over 16 years and holding various leadership positions, he started his own practice as a Management Consultant and has been working with several Indian and global brands since then.

In 2002, Mr Mishra joined hands with some of his like-minded friends to set up Smile Foundation. He has spearheaded the scaling up of the organization since the inception, helping it evolve from an idea to an Indian social institution of repute.

Currently, he is playing the role of a mentor to the management team at Smile Foundation. His leadership and vision as a social change-maker have been widely recognized by several forums including the Social Impact Leaders & Change Makers Award by Business World, Sustainability Leader of the Year by UBS Forums & IBM, Bharatiya Manavata Vikas Puraskar from the then President of India late  Shri Pranab Mukherjee, and the Odisha Living Legend Award by Odisha Diary. He is also a regular contributor to esteemed publications including the Economic Times, Business World, Times of India and Indian Express

Mr. Puneet Bali

Puneet has a rich experience of over 25 years marked by remarkable achievements and impactful leadership in the fields of banking, sustainable development, fundraising, marketing, and communications. He has led organisations including Care India, Helpage India, Dainik Bhaskar Group and Citi India. Throughout his career, Puneet has consistently demonstrated a keen ability to drive positive change and achieve significant milestones. As the Executive Director at Smile, he envisions building it as an impact institution of excellence in India in the coming years. Puneet loves to read, and enjoys cooking and gardening in his free time.

Mr. Subroto Roy

Subroto Roy has done his BE (Civil) from the National Institute of Technology followed by PGDM from the Xavier Institute of Management. He has an experience of more than 24 years from organizations such as Development Alternatives, Educomp, Wish Foundation and Tara Livelihood Academy. His journey with Smile Foundation started with leading the programmes division and he is now part of the operations and management team.
