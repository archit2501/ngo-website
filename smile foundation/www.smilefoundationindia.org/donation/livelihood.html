Your Donation Can Give Someone a Dignified Livelihood

A country like India, with a huge young population, can reap tremendous benefits from its demographic dividend if its youth are better skilled and employable. According to estimates, India’s demographic dividend window opened in 2005-06 and is expected to last until the mid-2050s. By 2047, India will have 1.1 billion people in the working age group (15-64), representing the peak of this demographic dividend. The next two decades are crucial for India to leverage this opportunity for economic growth.

Smile Foundation, recognizing the urgency of the moment, has reprogrammed its employability training initiative, STeP, to tackle the challenges posed by poverty and unemployment. Our campaign, ‘Tayyari Kal Ki’, focuses on training and up-skilling youth between the ages of 18-32 years, empowering them to secure sustainable livelihoods and build brighter futures.

This livelihood mission aligns with the Government of India’s ‘Skill India’ initiative. Currently, the programme is operational through 74 centres across 7 states. Over the last 15 years, Smile Foundation has trained more than 90,000 youth and successfully placed over 56,500 of them in reputable companies across India.

But the need is immense, and we cannot do this alone. Your support can make a difference. By donating to Smile Foundation’s ‘Tayyari Kal Ki’ campaign, you will help provide essential skill training, employment opportunities, and dignified livelihoods to underprivileged youth. Every contribution counts—your donation can lift families out of poverty and build a stronger, more prosperous India.

Empower the youth today. Donate now and be a part of India’s growth story.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Smile Foundation's livelihood programme, STeP empower underserved youth?

Smile Foundation's livelihood programme, STeP empowers underserved youth by providing them with vocational training and skill development opportunities. By acquiring market-relevant skills, they gain the confidence and ability to secure sustainable employment or start their own businesses, thereby breaking the cycle of poverty. ready for the workforce.

How does Smile Foundation ensure that the skills acquired by beneficiaries are relevant to the job market?

Smile Foundation conducts market research and collaborates with industry partners to identify the skills that are in demand in the job market. The courses are designed based on these findings, ensuring that the skills acquired by the beneficiaries are aligned with the current requirements of employers. This approach increases the employability of programme participants and enhances their chances of finding suitable employment.

How does Smile Foundation assess the impact and effectiveness of its livelihood programme?

Smile Foundation employs a robust monitoring and evaluation framework to assess the impact and effectiveness of its livelihood programme. This includes tracking employment outcomes, income improvement, success, and overall socio-economic upliftment of beneficiaries. Regular assessments, surveys, and feedback mechanisms are used to gather data and measure the programme's impact, which helps in refining and improving it over time.

How can I, as an individual donor, contribute specifically to the livelihood programme and make a tangible difference in someone's life?

As an individual donor, you can contribute to the livelihood programme by making financial contributions that directly support the training and skill development of beneficiaries. Your donations can be utilized for procuring training equipment, organizing workshops and capacity-building activities. Your contribution will make a tangible difference by enabling underserved youth to gain the skills they need to secure a better future for themselves and their families.

Is Rs. 500 too small an amount to make a donation?

Any amount donated to a noble cause is never too small. Our main vision is to imbibe a civic-driven change mentality in all of our stakeholders. Even if you donate Rs. 100 for any of our impact areas, it will be a big amount for us and we will express deep gratitude for your thoughtfulness and meaningful action.

Adam Khan

Adam Khan is a 23-year-old boy who lives in Govandi with his family. His father is an auto-rickshaw driver and the only earning person in his house. His father has irregular income which is sometimes as low as 500 rupees/day. Adam tried hard to find a job alongside pursuing his B. Com education but failed due to his low confidence and poor English.

In the month of Feb 2021, he came to know about Smile Twin e-learning Programme and about getting placement opportunities. He enrolled himself for the course.

After successfully completing the course, Adam got placed at Kotak Mahindra Bank as A Customer Service Officer where he earns 18000/-p.m. He and his family members are very happy for his achievements.

Adam khan Says "I took every opportunity to learn life skills through this training course. I am very thankful to SMILE FOUNDATION for showing me the right path and guiding me.

Gunjan

Gunjan is a 19-year-old ambitious girl from Bihar and is currently residing in Noida along with her parents and 3 siblings. She is pursuing her graduation in Political Science; however, she was always inclined to make a future in Digital Marketing. She joined STeP Digital Marketing Skilling Programme in Nov’ 20 during the mobilization drive. The STeP center not only gave her a platform to display her thoughts before likeminded people, but also made her realize her true potential. She is currently working at Forward Eye Technology as a Senior Digital Marketing Executive and grossing in a package of Rs. 12,000 per month.

Moumita Hazra

Moumita is 26 year old girl staying at Patipukur Northern part of Kolkata. She enrolled for Smile STeP training to upskill and to find an appropriate job. With her sincere efforts and on completion of her course she got placed at Sangha Netralaya as General Duty Assistant and is now earning a salary of Rs. 9000/- per month.

Moumita believes that this job will help her in achieving her dreams. She has taken the entire responsibility of her family now and is ensuring her brother continues his education.

Sahil

Sahil is an 18-year-old boy who lives with his parents in Delhi. He had to discontinue his studies due to financial problems in the family. He enrolled for the STeP training programme on recommendation of the mobilization staff from the centre. After completing his course, he joined Fortis Hospital, Shalimar Bagh as a General Duty Assistant. He is satisfied with his current job and is earning a salary of Rs. 15000/- per month.
